Biography

Childhood

When Hercules was born, his stepmother, Hera, was jealous. As a result, she attempted to kill Hercules shortly after he was born. She sent 2 snakes into his crib to kill him. However, she underestimated Hercules’ strength, as he strangled the snakes before they were able to kill him.

Early Adulthood

She may have been unsuccessful this time, but she did not stop trying to make his life miserable. When Hercules was a young adult with his own wife and children, Hera cast a spell on Hercules that drove him to kill his family.

12 Labors of Hercules

When he was freed from the spell, he was faced with immense guilt and heartbreak. This inspired him to find Apollo, the God of Truth, to whom he begged to be punished for his actions. Apollo ordered him to complete 12 tasks or “labors” for the Mycenaen king, Eurystheus. These became known as the 12 Labors of Hercules. Apollo declared that once these labors were completed, Hercules would be free of guilt and would achieve immortality. To read more about each of the 12 Labors, navigate to the “12 Labors of Hercules” page of the website.

Death

After Hercules completed his 12 tasks, he got remarried to a woman named Deianira. During their marriage, a centaur attempted to steal Deianira from Hercules. Hercules ended up shooting him with a poisonous arrow. As the centaur lay dying, he realized his blood was now poisonous and gave Deianira a blood-stained tunic, claiming it would keep her forever binded to Hercules. Years later, Deianira heard rumors that Hercules was having an affair. In an effort to bring him back to her, she gave him the blood-stained tunic to wear. What she didn’t realize, however, was that the tunic was poisonous. Hercules ended up dying as a result of this. After his death, he was taken to Olympus to spend the rest of eternity with the Gods.
