The Nemean Lion

For his first labor, Hercules was sent to the hills of Nemea, where a lion was terrorizing the people. Hercules was tasked with killing this lion in order. To do so, he trapped the lion in a cave and strangled it. He wore the animal’s pelt as a cloak for the remainder of his life.

The Lernaean Hydra

For his next task, Hercules was sent to Lerna, where a nine-headed, poisonous snake called Hydra who lived underwater was blocking the entrance to the Underworld. The issue, however, was that when one of the heads was cut off, a new head would grow back. Therefore, Hercules would need help. With the help of his nephew, Iolaus, Hercules was able to sever each of the 9 heads while Iolaus torched each wound, preventing a new head from growing back.

The Golden Hind

This next task did not include Hercules slaying any beasts. Instead, he was sent to the goddess Diana, where he was tasked with stealing her sacred pet. It was a red deer with golden antlers and bronze hooves. Eurystheus believed that Diana would kill anyone who tried to come for her precious pet. However, he underestimated Hercules’ charm because as soon as Hercules explained his situation to Diana, she let him go on his way with no repercussions.

The Erymanthean Boar

For his 4th task, Hercules was sent to Mount Erymanthus to kill a man-eating boar. Using a giant net to trap the boar, he was successful.

The Augean Stables

Eurystheus chose this next task because he believed it to be humiliating as well as impossible. Hercules was instructed with cleaning all 3,000+ of King Augeas’ horse stables in one day. Hercules, being determined to complete all 12 labors and being as clever as he was, realized this was impossible to do by hand. This forced him to get creative. He built a dam, causing a river to run through the stables, cleaning it in no time.

The Stymphlaian Birds

The 6th labor included Hercules going to the town of Stymphalos and driving away a huge flock of carnivorous birds that had been occupying the town’s trees for some time. To accomplish this, he had the assistance of the goddess Athena. She provided him with a magical noisemaker that was made by another god, Hephaistos. Using this tool, Hercules had no troubles scaring the birds away.

The Cretan Bull

Hercules was then sent to Crete to capture a bull that had impregnated the king of the island’s wife, Pasiphae. He successfully captured the bull, turned it over to Eurystheus, who let it free in Marathon, Greece. Pasiphae ended up giving birth to a creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man.

The Horses of Diomedes

For his 8th labor, Hercules was sent to capture the 4 man-eating horses that were terrorizing the Thracian king, Diomedes. He was successful and brought them to Eurystheus, who gave them to Hera.

Hippolyte's Belt

This labor, seemingly easy at first, ended up being more trouble for Hercules than he anticipated. He was instructed to steal the belt from the Amazon queen Hippolyte. She initially agreed to give him the belt with no trouble, but Hera spread a rumor that Hercules was going to kidnap Hippolyte. The women of the Amazon attacked Hercules as a result of this rumor. Fearing for his life, he killed Hippolyte and ripped the belt from her body.

The Cattle of Geryon

Next, Hercules was sent to Africa to steal the cattle of the three-headed, six-legged monster called Geryon. Hera interfered as much as she could to prevent Hercules from being successful, but nevertheless, he succeeded and returned to Mycenae with the cattle.

The Apples of Hesperides

For his second-to-last labor, Hercules was instructed to steal Hera’s wedding gift to Zeus: a set of golden apples. These apples were heavily guarded by a group of nymphs called the Hesperides. This task proved very difficult for Hercules, and he was only successful after getting help from Prometheus and Atlas. He got away with the apples, showed them to Eurystheus, and then returned them to the gods’ garden where they belonged.

Cerberus

For his final challenge, Hercules was sent to Hades to kidnap Cerberus, the aggressive and ferocious three-headed dog that guarded the gates. He used his immense strength to wrestle and restrain the dog, who was then returned unharmed to his post at the entrance of the Underworld.
